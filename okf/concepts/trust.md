## Specification

### Definition

Trust in a Synthetic Team is the measurable outcome of visible reasoning, explicit trade-offs, and distributed accountability. It is not a personality attribute — it emerges because decisions can be inspected, compared, and challenged without relying on charisma or positional authority.

### Trust Equation

```
Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) ÷ Self-Orientation
```

| Component | Source |
|---|---|
| Credibility | Visible reasoning and explicit trade-offs |
| Reliability | Tracked commitments and closed loops |
| Intimacy | Reduced reliance on positional authority |
| Low Self-Orientation | Role constraints limiting personal bias |

### Trust Mechanisms

1. Decisions are recorded with alternatives, criteria, and rationale
2. Recommendations include explicit trade-offs and comparison against alternatives
3. Role separation prevents any single actor from controlling both creation and validation
4. Every output is traceable to its producing role and authority

---

## Rationale

Trust is not an input to a Synthetic Team — it is an output. The system does not ask participants to trust each other; it asks them to follow a process that produces trustworthy results. When every recommendation answers "why this path," "what criteria were applied," and "how was it compared against alternatives," trust becomes a property of the system, not the individuals within it.
